Digs - Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 4, 2026

Overview

Digs is a mobile application that lets you browse your Discogs vinyl collection offline. Your privacy matters, and this policy explains what data the app accesses and how it is handled.

Data we collect

We do not collect any personal data.

Digs does not have its own servers. The app does not send any data to us and does not include analytics, crash reporting, or advertising.

Data stored on your device

The following data is stored locally on your device and never leaves it:

  • Discogs OAuth credentials — stored securely in your device’s keychain (iOS) or encrypted storage (Android) to authenticate with the Discogs API.
  • Collection data — your folders, releases, and related metadata are synced from Discogs and stored in a local database for offline access.
  • Cached images — album artwork is cached locally so you can browse your collection without an internet connection.

Third-party services

Digs communicates directly with the Discogs API to authenticate your account and retrieve your collection. This connection is governed by Discogs’ Privacy Policy. No other third-party services are used.

Data sharing

We do not share, sell, or transfer any of your data to third parties.

Data deletion

You can delete all data stored by Digs at any time by logging out within the app or by uninstalling it. This removes all locally stored credentials, collection data, and cached images.

Children’s privacy

Digs is not directed at children under 13 and does not knowingly collect any information from children.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. Any changes will be reflected on this page with an updated date.
